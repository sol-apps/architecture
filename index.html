How solhann.net Works

This is a tiny workshop for making little websites. Below is the whole thing explained with no jargon — just pictures and plain words. Give it a scroll.

THE BIG IDEA

One idea → one live website

Every little app here — a quiz, a gallery, an expense tracker — lives at its own web address, like quiz.solhann.net. They all share one small rented computer on the internet (we call it the server), but each app behaves as if it has the place to itself.

The whole point of this workshop is that going from “I have an idea” to “it’s live on the internet” takes one step and about half a minute. Here’s how that trip actually happens.

THE JOURNEY

Watch a change go live

When something changes — you tweak a colour, fix a typo — that change takes a short automated journey. You press “send” once; robots do the rest.

You make a change
on your own computer A “post office”
catches it & checks it (GitHub) The building
receives the delivery (the server) The website
updates for everyone

The delivery van has a very narrow job: it can only drop parcels at the loading dock. It can’t wander the building, open other doors, or take anything out. So even the automated helpers have almost no power — by design.

STAYING SAFE

Every app in its own locked room

The apps share one building, but each one gets its own locked room. An app can only ever touch its own things. If one app misbehaves — or someone tries something sneaky — it can’t reach into the room next door.

OWN KEY

Each room has its own lock and key. No shared master key means no app can quietly read another app’s data.

OWN LIMITS

Each room gets a fixed slice of power. A greedy app can’t hog the whole building or slow everyone else down.

APPS THAT REMEMBER

A filing cabinet with two doors

Some apps need to remember things — your saved receipts, your high score. Those apps get a private filing cabinet. It has two separate doors, and that separation is what keeps your data both usable and safe.

FRONT DOOR · VISITORS

Anyone using the app comes in the front. Strict rules mean they only ever see their own stuff — never anyone else’s.

BACK DOOR · THE MAKER

You send the app’s behind-the-scenes instructions through a separate back door — kept well away from the visitors and the data.

ALL TOGETHER

Every app on one shelf

The moment a new app goes live, it automatically appears on the front page — a gallery of everything in the workshop. Nobody adds it by hand; the shelf notices its new neighbour and puts up a card.

And this page you’re reading? It’s just another app on that same shelf, built and shipped exactly the way this whole tour describes.
